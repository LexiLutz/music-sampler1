Frank Sinatra

Frank Sinatra was born in December of 1915 and died in May of 1998. He was born in Hoboken, New Jersey, and met his end in Los Angeles, California. When described, he was talked about as a singer, actor, and producer. He won several Grammys in various categories and was named one of the top 100 most influential people of the 20th century. He began pairing with another man to sing. They were mildly successful, but he wanted a solo career.

Mr. Sinatra kept singing well into his later years. Where some artists loose their voice as the years pass, he was certainly an exception. In the audio sample below of the song New York, New York, Sinatra performed it live in Madison Square Garden in 1974. He is still regularly played by millions, especially during the holidays. The rest is history. This information was published by A&E Television Networks.
